What supplement buyers should clarify first

The same botanical ingredient can behave differently in capsules, tablets, gummies, stick packs, and blended powders. Buyers should define the intended format, target grade, document requirement, and review stage before comparing suppliers.

  • Target marker, ratio, assay, or grade language.
  • Capsule, tablet, gummy, powder, softgel, or blend context.
  • COA/TDS and any testing files needed for QA review.
  • Sample quantity, first-order estimate, MOQ, packing, and destination.

Useful first RFQ context

Send product name, target specification, dosage form, application notes, first-order estimate, sample need, and whether your team is screening, validating, or preparing a purchase order. That turns a generic ingredient question into a supplier response that procurement and QA can compare.

Buyer workflow

Build the supplement inquiry around format, grade, and QA path

Supplement buyers usually need one supplier response that can serve procurement, QA, formulation, and commercialization. A useful inquiry should make the project stage clear: early ingredient screen, sample review, replacement supplier search, pilot batch, or first purchase order planning.

Format

Dosage form fit

Capsules, tablets, gummies, stick packs, and powders each create different questions around grade, carrier, handling, color, odor, and sensory expectations.

Spec

Marker clarity

Confirm whether the project requires a marker-standardized extract, ratio extract, whole powder, mushroom extract, or specialty active.

QA

Document review

Ask whether COA/TDS can be routed for screening, sample approval, supplier onboarding, or purchase order release.

Supply

Commercial route

Include sample quantity, first-order estimate, destination, packing preference, and whether U.S.-side fulfillment matters.

Copy-ready supplement ingredient RFQ template

Hello Essence Source team, we are reviewing [ingredient name] for a [capsule / tablet / gummy / powder / stick pack / blend] supplement project. Our target specification is [marker, ratio, assay, extract type, or grade], with estimated first order of [quantity] and destination [city, state/country]. Please advise sample path, MOQ, packing, lead time, and COA/TDS availability for [screening / sample review / first PO planning].

Common issues to avoid

  • Comparing prices before the target specification is fixed.
  • Requesting documents without naming the dosage form or review stage.
  • Mixing extract, powder, and standardized active options in one quote comparison.
  • Leaving out destination, sample quantity, or first-order planning details.
  • Using consumer-facing claim language instead of technical sourcing criteria.
