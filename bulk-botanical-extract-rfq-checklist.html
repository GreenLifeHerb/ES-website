Bulk Botanical Extract RFQ Checklist

Bulk extract quotes become more useful when specification, order size, document needs, packing, sample timing, and delivery assumptions are stated before the supplier prepares a response.

Send RFQ Bulk extracts page

1. Define the material

Include botanical name, part used, extraction ratio or marker, testing method if required, and any particle size, carrier, solubility, or sensory limits that affect formulation.

2. Define the commercial path

Provide target order size, expected annual volume, destination state or country, desired packing, sample quantity, and whether the order is urgent or part of future planning.

3. Define the review files

State whether your team needs COA, TDS, representative test files, or sample-specific documents before issuing a purchase order.

Quote-ready RFQ template

Hello Essence Source team, we are requesting a quote for [ingredient name] with target specification [marker, extract ratio, grade, carrier, solubility, or method requirement]. Our intended application is [supplement, beverage, food, cosmetic, or other B2B use]. Please advise sample path, MOQ, estimated lead time, packing options, and COA/TDS availability for [first order quantity] shipping to [destination]. We are evaluating this for [new project, reformulation, supplier replacement, or production planning].

What to decide before comparing prices

  • Whether the target specification is fixed or still flexible.
  • Whether the buyer needs a sample before final quote comparison.
  • Whether U.S.-side fulfillment, replenishment, or import timing matters.
  • Whether the team needs product-level TDS, lot-oriented COA, or both.
  • Whether the quote supports pilot review, first PO, or repeat-order planning.

What makes a bulk quote unreliable?

Quotes become hard to compare when the buyer only asks for "best price" without a target specification, order quantity, or document requirement. A lower unit price may not help if the grade, lead time, packing, or COA path does not fit the buyer's qualification process.

  • Unclear marker or ratio language.
  • No target MOQ, order size, or annual planning signal.
  • No application context for supplement, food, beverage, or cosmetic use.
  • No clarity on whether U.S. stock or replenishment timing matters.

Best RFQ format

Send one concise message with product name, specification, quantity, destination, packing preference, sample need, and document need. If your team has an internal spec sheet, attach it or paste the relevant limits into the message.

RFQ stages

Different quote stages need different detail

A first screening request should be concise. A production quote should be more complete. Separating those stages helps both sides avoid premature assumptions about price, stock, and documentation.

Stage 01

Supplier screen

Confirm category fit, specification range, document path, and whether the supplier can discuss the target ingredient in B2B sourcing terms.

Stage 02

Sample review

Align sample quantity, grade, intended test or formulation question, and the documents needed for internal review.

Stage 03

First PO quote

Confirm order size, packing, lead time, destination, payment and shipping assumptions, and document routing before order planning.

Stage 04

Repeat supply

Discuss annual volume, replenishment rhythm, specification stability, and whether future lots need the same document review logic.
