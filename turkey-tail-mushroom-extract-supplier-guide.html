Supplier questions buyers should ask first

Turkey tail is often reviewed in mushroom blend and wellness programs, so buyers need clear specification language and sample context. A supplier response should connect identity, grade, COA/TDS availability, MOQ, and lead time.

  • Is the material fruiting body, mycelium, or a defined blend?
  • What polysaccharide, beta-glucan, or ratio language is tied to the grade?
  • Can documents support screening, sample review, or first PO planning?
  • Does the supplier understand mushroom blend and powder-format review?

What makes supplier responses easier to compare

Ask each supplier to answer the same material identity, specification, sample, MOQ, packing, destination, and document questions. This keeps the comparison tied to the same commercial case instead of a loose ingredient name.

Keep the COA/TDS request tied to the same grade

For the most useful supplier response, send the target specification, intended application, first-order estimate, sample quantity, destination, and the exact document question your QA team needs answered. This keeps the quote, sample, and COA/TDS path aligned.
