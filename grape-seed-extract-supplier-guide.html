How to Choose a Grape Seed Extract Supplier

Grape seed extract sourcing should align Vitis vinifera identity, proanthocyanidin specification, test method language, sample review, and COA/TDS path before supplier comparison.

Supplier questions buyers should ask first

Grape seed extract is often compared by proanthocyanidin level and method language. Buyers should clarify whether the quote, sample, and document path refer to the same grade and commercial route.

  • What proanthocyanidin level is tied to the grade?
  • Is UV or another method language part of the specification?
  • Can COA/TDS be routed for sample or lot review?
  • Is the application capsule, tablet, powder blend, beverage, or cosmetic-facing?

Best RFQ context

Send target specification, intended application, first-order volume, sample need, packing preference, destination, and document requirements. This helps the supplier provide a quote that procurement and QA can actually compare.

Keep the COA/TDS request tied to the same grade

For the most useful supplier response, send the target specification, intended application, first-order estimate, sample quantity, destination, and the exact document question your QA team needs answered. This keeps the quote, sample, and COA/TDS path aligned.

Buyer comparison snapshot

Ask whether the grape seed offer is built around OPC, polyphenols, or broad extract language

Grape seed extract buyers should confirm the quoted marker language before comparing suppliers. OPC, proanthocyanidin, polyphenol, and ratio language may not describe the same grade or method. A clean RFQ should connect Vitis vinifera identity, specification, testing language, application, sample review, and COA/TDS availability in one request.
