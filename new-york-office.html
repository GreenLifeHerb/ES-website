Commercial account support for U.S. ingredient buyers

Buyers usually contact Essence Source when they need a clear next step: quote review, sample planning, document routing, or supplier qualification. Our sales support workflow is built to collect the information needed for a useful reply instead of sending buyers through generic catalog follow-up.

  • RFQ routing: Product, grade, quantity, destination, and timing are reviewed before quote assumptions are confirmed.
  • Document coordination: COA/TDS requests are tied to product path, review stage, and buyer application.
  • Sample support: Sample requests are routed around the exact ingredient, specification, and intended internal review.
  • Commercial clarity: MOQ, lead time, packing, and fulfillment route are confirmed by inquiry rather than assumed from a public page.

Supporting supplement, beverage, food, and personal-care teams

Different buyer teams need different answers. A supplement brand may focus first on assay and COA/TDS review, while a beverage team may ask about solubility, appearance, and sample handling. A cosmetic or personal-care buyer may need identity and documentation clarity before supplier approval.

  • Specification alignment: Botanical name, part used, marker, ratio, and test method are clarified before commercial comparison.
  • Application context: Dosage form and use case help determine which questions should be answered first.
  • Repeat supply planning: Commercial volume, destination, and timing are separated from early sample review so buyers can compare suppliers more accurately.

Does the New York office hold physical product inventory?

This page is for commercial communication and sourcing coordination. Physical sample routes and bulk fulfillment paths should be confirmed by inquiry for the exact product, grade, destination, and timing.

What is the response turnaround for wholesale price quotes?

Quote timing depends on product complexity, specification, volume, document requirements, and whether follow-up clarification is needed. Clear RFQ details make the first response faster and more useful.

What to send before a commercial support discussion

The most useful first message gives the sourcing desk enough context to identify the right internal path. If your team is still early in the process, say that. If the project is already quote-ready, include the commercial assumptions so we can avoid basic follow-up loops.

Product Ingredient and grade

Share product name, target specification, marker, ratio, or custom requirement.

Commercial Quantity and destination

Include sample, pilot, first PO, or repeat-order context when known.

QA Document needs

Clarify whether you need COA/TDS review before sample, quote, or purchase approval.
